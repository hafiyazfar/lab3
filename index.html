Dataran Merdeka, Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia

Dataran Merdeka, or Independence Square, is one of the most emblematic and historic sites in Kuala Lumpur.It marks the place where Malaysia’s independence was declared in 1957, making it a site of profound national significance. The square is surrounded by beautifully preserved colonial buildings, including the Sultan Abdul Samad Building, and offers a spacious green field where the British used to play cricket. Today, Dataran Merdeka serves as the focal point for national celebrations and public events, such as the annual Merdeka Parade. The towering flagpole, one of the world’s tallest, proudly displaying the Malaysian flag, is a symbol of the nation’s sovereignty and pride.
